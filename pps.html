Pozzolanic Performance Score (PPS)

ASTM C618 judges a pozzolan on a single number — the 28-day Strength Activity Index — as a pass/fail at 75%. That ignores durability entirely. The Pozzolanic Performance Score combines four normalized metrics (compressive SAI, total water absorption, capillary sorptivity, and sulfate mass change) into one 0–100 index. Each metric is scored relative to the best-performing calcined sample at the selected curing age, then weighted. Adjust the weights to explore how the ranking responds to different priorities.

How the score is calculated

Each metric is measured in different units and points in a different direction — strength is "higher is better", while absorption, sorptivity, and sulfate mass change are "lower is better". To combine them, every metric is rescaled to a common 0–100 range using min–max normalization within the calcined samples at the chosen age, with the three durability metrics inverted so that 100 always means best.

PPS = w₁·S̃ + w₂·Ã + w₃·C̃ + w₄·Ū
S̃ — compressive SAI, scaled 0–100 (higher = better)
Ã — total water absorption, inverted and scaled (lower = better)
C̃ — capillary sorptivity, inverted and scaled (lower = better)
Ū — sulfate mass change (absolute, 56-day), inverted and scaled (less change = better)
Weights w₁–w₄ are set above and sum to 1. SAI is referenced to C1 (plain OPC) at the matching age: 47.7 MPa at 28 days, 52.9 MPa at 90 days. The 28- and 90-day scores are normalized separately so each reflects performance at that age.

Because the two ages are scored independently, the ranking shifts with curing time: the K5 series leads early through low absorption and sorptivity, while the slower-reacting K1 series overtakes it by 90 days as continued pozzolanic reaction refines the pore structure — a crossover a single 28-day index cannot capture.
